About Catfish

Catfish are a favorite among bottom fishermen in the Salt River. These bottom-dwelling fish are known for their whisker-like barbels and their ability to grow to impressive sizes.

Channel catfish and flathead catfish are the primary species found in the Salt River. Catfish are most active at night and prefer murky, slow-moving waters.

Best Fishing Spots: Look for catfish in the deep, slow-moving sections of the river, especially near log jams, undercut banks, and areas with soft muddy bottoms.

Best Bait: Catfish are attracted to strong-smelling baits such as chicken liver, stink bait, and night crawlers. Cut bait and live minnows are also effective.

Catfish Activity by Season in the Salt River

Season Catfish Activity Level Notes
Spring (Mar-May) Moderate Warming water increases activity; good for pre-spawn feeding
Summer (Jun-Aug) High Warm water and longer nights make catfish very active feeders
Fall (Sep-Nov) High Cooler temperatures and shorter days keep catfish feeding actively
Winter (Dec-Feb) Low Cold water slows metabolism; best during mild winter days
